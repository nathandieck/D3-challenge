Relationship between Smoking Rates and Income

The data suggest the existence of a slight but noticable inverse correlation between a state's typical income (median is assumed but not explicitly specified in the dataset) and smoking rates. In other words, the lower one's personal income, the more likely one is to smoke. It is unclear whether one directly causes the other (and which direction the causation would occur), or whether a confound such as education level influences both variables.
